Japanese Writing System

Japnese is written with three different writing systems:

Hiragana Katakana Kanji


Hiragana and Katakana are collectively called kana

かkaなna

仮名

There are 46 Hiragana symbols and 46 Katakana symbols.

Hiragana and Katakana can be comparable to the difference between English capital and lowercase letters. Capital letters and lowercase letters are fundamentally the same sound but serve different functions in written language.

Capital letters and lowercase letters are two sides of the same coin. Similarly, Hiragana and Katakana are two sides of the same coin.

For example:

English: 'A' = 'a'

Japanese: あa = ア　a

Japanese Writing System

Japnese is written with three different writing systems:

Hiragana Katakana Kanji

Hiragana and Katakana are collectively called kana. We will address kanji at a later time.

かkaなna

仮名

There are 46 Hiragana symbols and 46 Katakana symbols.

Hiragana and Katakana can be comparable to the difference between English capital and lowercase letters. Capital letters and lowercase letters are fundamentally the same sound but serve different functions in written language.

Capital letters and lowercase letters are two sides of the same coin. Similarly, Hiragana and Katakana are two sides of the same coin.

For example:

English: A = a ・ I = i ・ E = e

Japanese: あa=ア 　a・い 　i=イ　i・え　e=エ　e

"What is the difference between the two kana systems?"

Native Japanese words are written in Hiragana
Foreign/Loan words are written in Katakana

Example:

Hiragana

どうも

'Thank you; Thanks'


Katakana

オレンジジュース

'Orange juice'

ひらがなhi ra ga na and カタカナka ta ka na

Hiragana is rounder than Katakana. Katakana uses more straight lines with some curved lines. Hiragana is more 'cursive' like and Katakana is much more 'rigid' in comparison.

Some kana look almost identical in hiragana and katakana form! Similar to how some letters in English look almost identical in their capital and lowercase form!

Katakana is also used for sound effects and onomatopoeias. Native words that are usually written in Hiragana can be written in Katakana to emphasize the word, or for stylistic reasons.

IT'S LIKE IF I WROTE IN ALL CAPS. WRITING IN KATAKANA CAN MAKE THINGS VISUALLY POP OUT!

ドドドドドdo do do do do = In JoJo's Bizarre Adventure, it indicates something bizarre is about to happen


ゴゴゴgo go go = Also in Jojo's, indicates something 'menacing'
